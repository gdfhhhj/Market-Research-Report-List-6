<p><p>HVDC converter stations are critical in the transmission of electricity over long distances. The market types include monopolar systems, which use a single conductor and ground return, making them simple yet less efficient. Bipolar systems utilize two conductors, improving efficiency and reliability. Back-to-back stations connect two AC systems directly, facilitating power flow between them. Multi-terminal systems allow multiple connection points, enhancing flexibility and integration of renewable energy sources. Each type caters to specific transmission needs and operational requirements.</p></p>
