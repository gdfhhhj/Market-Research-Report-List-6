<p><p>Adipose Derived Stem Cell (ADSC) therapy involves utilizing stem cells harvested from adipose tissue for regenerative medicine. The market is segmented into two main types: autologous and allogeneic stem cells. Autologous stem cells are sourced from the patient's own fat tissue, minimizing rejection risks and enhancing compatibility. In contrast, allogeneic stem cells are obtained from donors, allowing for broader availability but potentially increasing the risk of immune rejection. Both types offer unique advantages in various therapeutic applications.</p></p>
