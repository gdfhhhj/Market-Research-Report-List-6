<p><p>The denatured alcohol market primarily consists of two types: Specially Denatured Alcohol (SDA) and Completely Denatured Alcohol (CDA). SDA is treated with specific additives to make it unfit for human consumption while allowing certain uses, such as in cosmetics or solvents. In contrast, CDA is rendered undrinkable through a more robust formulation, typically used in industrial applications and as a fuel. Both types serve distinct purposes across sectors like pharmaceuticals, personal care, and manufacturing, reflecting their diverse commercial applications.</p></p>
