<p><p>Alzheimer's drugs are categorized into three main types: cholinergic drugs, memantine, and combined drugs. Cholinergic drugs enhance acetylcholine levels to improve cognitive function, targeting memory and learning. Memantine works by regulating glutamate to protect brain cells from excessive stimulation, often used in moderate to severe cases. Combined drugs integrate these approaches, offering synergistic effects for better overall management of symptoms. This diverse market aims to address different stages and aspects of Alzheimer’s disease, enhancing patient care and quality of life.</p></p>
