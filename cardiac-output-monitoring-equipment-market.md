<p><p>Cardiac output monitoring equipment is crucial for assessing heart function and guiding treatment. It is categorized into three types: </p><p>1. **Invasive**: This method involves direct measurement through catheters inserted into the bloodstream, providing precise data but carrying higher risks. </p><p>2. **Minimally Invasive**: This approach uses techniques that require small incisions or less intrusive methods, balancing accuracy with a lower risk of complications. </p><p>3. **Non-Invasive**: Utilizing external sensors and imaging techniques, this method offers safe and convenient monitoring without penetrating the skin, though it may be less precise. Each type serves unique clinical needs.</p></p>
