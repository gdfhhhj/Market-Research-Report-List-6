<p><p>The quantum computing market primarily consists of two types: gate-level quantum computers and quantum annealers. Gate-level quantum computers utilize quantum bits (qubits) for complex operations, making them suitable for tasks requiring high computational power, like cryptography and optimization. In contrast, quantum annealers are designed to solve optimization problems by finding the lowest energy state of a system, often used in logistics and material science. Each type targets specific applications, shaping the evolving landscape of quantum technology.</p></p>
