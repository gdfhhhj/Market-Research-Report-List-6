<p><p>The Interstitial Free (IF) sheet steel market is primarily divided into two types: hot rolled and cold rolled. Hot rolled IF sheet steel is produced at high temperatures, resulting in improved ductility and formability, making it suitable for large-scale manufacturing. In contrast, cold rolled IF sheet steel undergoes a reduction process at room temperature, offering enhanced surface finish and tighter tolerances, ideal for applications requiring precision. Both types cater to various industries, including automotive and construction, emphasizing strength and weight reduction.</p></p>
