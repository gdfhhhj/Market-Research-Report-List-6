<p><p>Neuromarketing technology encompasses various advanced methods to study consumer behavior and preferences. Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood flow, offering insights into emotional responses. Electroencephalography (EEG) records electrical activity in the brain, providing real-time data on cognitive processes. Eye tracking monitors where and how long consumers focus visually, revealing attention patterns. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) traces metabolic processes, while Magnetoencephalography (MEG) maps magnetic field changes in the brain, aiding in understanding consumer decision-making. Other methods include biophysiological measures like facial coding and implicit response tests.</p></p>
