<p><p>SPE cartridges are widely used in chromatography to separate compounds of interest from a mixture. Reversed-phase cartridges are suitable for nonpolar compounds, while ion-exchange cartridges are designed for separating charged compounds based on their ionic properties. Normal phase cartridges separate compounds based on their polarity, making them ideal for polar compounds. Each type of market caters to specific chemical properties and interactions, providing researchers with a range of options for efficient compound separation and purification in various applications.</p></p>
