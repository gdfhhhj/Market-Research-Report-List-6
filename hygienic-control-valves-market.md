<p><p>Hygienic control valves consist of two main types of actuators: linear actuators and rotary actuators. Linear actuators operate by moving in a straight line, while rotary actuators operate by rotating around a central axis. Both types of actuators play a crucial role in controlling the flow of fluids in hygienic applications, such as in the food and pharmaceutical industries. Linear actuators are often used for precise control and shut-off functions, while rotary actuators are commonly used for throttling and regulating flow.</p></p>
