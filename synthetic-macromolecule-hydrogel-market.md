<p><p>The synthetic macromolecule hydrogel market is primarily categorized into physical and chemical hydrogels. Physical hydrogels are formed through non-covalent interactions such as hydrogen bonding, ionic interactions, and van der Waals forces, allowing for reversible and often tunable properties. In contrast, chemical hydrogels are created through covalent bonding, resulting in a more stable and robust structure. This distinction influences their applications in fields like healthcare, drug delivery, and tissue engineering, as each type offers unique characteristics suited for specific needs.</p></p>
