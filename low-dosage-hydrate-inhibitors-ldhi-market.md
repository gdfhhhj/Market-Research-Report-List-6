<p><p>Low Dosage Hydrate Inhibitors (LDHI) are chemicals used in the oil and gas industry to prevent the formation of hydrates during production. The market includes several types: Anti-Agglomerate (AA) inhibitors, which prevent hydrate particles from clumping together; Kinetic Hydrate Inhibitors (KHI), which delay hydrate formation by slowing down the nucleation and growth processes; and other specialized inhibitors that may include thermodynamic agents or polymer-based solutions. These products enhance flow assurance while minimizing chemical usage and costs.</p></p>
