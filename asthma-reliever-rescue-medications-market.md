<p><p>Asthma reliever medications, commonly known as rescue medications, are primarily categorized into quick-relief and long-term control medications. Quick-relief medications, such as short-acting beta-agonists, provide immediate relief from acute asthma symptoms and are essential during asthma attacks. In contrast, long-term control medications, including inhaled corticosteroids and leukotriene modifiers, are used daily to manage chronic asthma symptoms and prevent exacerbations. Together, these medications help maintain effective asthma management and improve overall respiratory function.</p></p>
